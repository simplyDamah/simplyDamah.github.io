About Us

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide a high-quality education that prepares students for success in a diverse and ever-changing world. We are committed to fostering a safe and supportive learning environment where all students can thrive.

Our History

Lenana School is a secondary school in Nairobi, Kenya. It was formed in 1949 by colonial governor Philip Euen Mitchell,
then as the Duke of York School, named after a British World War II King George V-class battleship (1939).

Our Values

We believe in a set of core values that guide our actions and decisions. These values include:

  • Integrity
  • Respect
  • Responsibility
  • Excellence
  • Community

Our Faculty

Our faculty consists of experienced and dedicated educators who are passionate about teaching and learning. They are committed to providing personalized attention and support to every student, helping them achieve their full potential.

Our Facilities

Our school boasts state-of-the-art facilities, including modern classrooms, well-equipped science and computer labs, a spacious library, and extensive sports facilities. These resources provide an optimal learning environment for our students.
